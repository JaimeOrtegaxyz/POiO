Chipotle & Media Crema Thighs
over Charred Elote Rice

Smoky and creamy, lime-bright, weeknight-fast. The chipotle does the heavy lifting; the media crema rounds it off. Char on the corn is non-negotiable.

Time15 prep + 35 total
Serves3
MethodStovetop + broiler
FlavorLatin / Mexican

What you need

Protein

  • 6 bone-in, skin-on chicken thighs

Veg & aromatics

  • 2 ears elote (or 1.5 cups frozen corn)
  • 1 white onion — half diced, half thin half-moons
  • 4 cloves garlic, smashed
  • 1 small red onion (for pickling)
  • 1 bunch cilantro
  • 3 limes

Pantry & spices

  • 2 tbsp chipotles in adobo (chopped) + 1 tsp adobo sauce
  • 1 cup long-grain white rice
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tsp Mexican oregano
  • 2 tbsp neutral oil
  • Kosher salt, black pepper

For the finish

  • 1/2 cup media crema
  • 1/3 cup cotija, crumbled low
  • Salsa macha (optional, drizzle)
  • Flaky salt

Method

  1. 1
    Pickle the onion first, walk away. Thin half-moons of red onion in a small bowl with the juice of 1 lime, a pinch of salt, a pinch of sugar. Let it sit while you cook — it’ll be neon pink and crunchy in 20 minutes.
  2. 2
    Season the thighs hard. Pat dry. Salt generously on both sides, then a dusting of cumin, oregano, and black pepper. Skin-side up, set aside.
  3. 3
    Char the elote dry. Hot, dry skillet. Strip the kernels off the cobs straight into the pan and don’t stir for 90 seconds. You want black spots, blistered edges, the smell of summer. Char it until it catches. Stir, char again, then dump into a bowl with a squeeze of lime.
  4. 4
    Build the rice. Same pan, 1 tbsp oil over medium. Diced white onion + garlic, cook until translucent and just starting to catch — 4 minutes. Add rice, toast 1 minute until it smells nutty and you can’t help leaning in. Pour in 1 3/4 cups water, salt, bring to a boil, cover, low heat 15 minutes. Off heat, rest 5. Fold the charred corn back in.
  5. 5
    Crisp the thighs. Heavy skillet (cast iron if you have it), 1 tbsp oil, screaming hot. Thighs skin-side down, press them flat for the first minute. Don’t move them for 7 minutes — you want the skin golden and crackling. Flip, 4 more minutes. Pull, rest skin-side up on a plate.
  6. 6
    Build the chipotle crema. Same pan, fat still in it, heat down. Half-moon white onion, soften 2 minutes. Off heat. Stir in the chopped chipotle + adobo, then the media crema. It’ll loosen with the rendered fat and turn the colour of a wet brick. Taste — squeeze of lime if it needs lift, salt if it needs salt.
  7. 7
    Broil to finish (optional but worth it). Return the thighs skin-up to the pan, slide under the broiler 90 seconds until the skin catches an extra char. Just watch it — broilers are personal.

The finish

Bed of elote rice. Thigh on top, skin-up. Spoon the chipotle crema around — not on the skin, you’ll soften it. Pile the pickled onion. Cotija crumble. Cilantro torn over generously. Salsa macha drizzle if you’ve got it. Final hard squeeze of lime, flaky salt, and eat immediately while the skin is still loud.

Pantry notes

  • Cotija is low — queso fresco crumbles work, or skip if you must.
  • No media crema? Equal parts Greek yoghurt + cream, or sour cream thinned with milk.
  • Keeps 3 days in the fridge. Reheat the rice with a splash of water, the thigh gently in a covered pan.
  • Leftovers: shred any remaining thigh into tomorrow’s tacos with the pickled onion.
  • This uses about 2 tbsp chipotles in adobo — roughly a third of the can. Still plenty.
