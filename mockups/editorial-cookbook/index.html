03 Recipe

Chipotle-Mango
Smashed Thighs

Boneless thighs smashed thin, lacquered in a chipotle-mango glaze until the edges catch and crackle. Served over rice with quick-pickled red onion, crema, and so much lime.

Vibe Smoky, sweet, sticky. A weeknight that feels like a Friday.
Time 15 min prep · 35 min total
Serves 2 hungry · 3 polite
Family Latin / Mexican · Smashing

What you need

Protein

  • 4 boneless chicken thighs (~600 g)

Veg & Aromatics

  • 1 ripe mango — peeled, diced
  • ½ red onion — sliced thin
  • 3 cloves garlic — smashed
  • 1 small bunch cilantro — picked
  • 2 limes — one juiced, one wedged

Pantry & Spice

  • 2 chipotles in adobo + 1 tbsp of the sauce (low)
  • 1 tsp Mexican oregano
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • Salt — generous
  • 1 cup jasmine rice

To Finish

  • Media crema — a drizzle
  • Crumbled queso fresco (low)
  • Flaky salt

Method

  1. 1
    Make the glaze. Throw the mango, chipotles + adobo, garlic, juice of one lime, honey, cumin, oregano, and a serious pinch of salt into a blender. Blitz until it looks like a sunset — thick, red-orange, glossy. Taste. It should hit you with smoke first, then sweetness, then heat that creeps up. Adjust salt.
  2. 2
    Smash the thighs. Lay thighs between two sheets of parchment. Pound with the bottom of a heavy pan until they're an even ½-inch thick — almost translucent at the edges. More surface area, more browning, more crackle.
  3. 3
    Marinate, briefly. Toss the smashed thighs in half the glaze. Let them sit for 10 minutes while you cook the rice. Reserve the other half — that's your finishing sauce.
  4. 4
    Quick-pickle the onion. Slivered red onion into a small bowl with the juice of half a lime, a pinch of salt, a pinch of sugar. Push it down so the lime covers everything. Forget about it until the end — it'll be pink and snapping by then.
  5. 5
    Sear hard. Cast iron, hot, a slick of oil. Lay thighs in flat — they should hiss. Don't crowd, work in two batches. 3 minutes one side until the glaze caramelizes and the edges catch black. Flip. 2 minutes the other side. Pull them out.
  6. 6
    Finish in the glaze. Off the heat, brush the reserved glaze over the resting thighs. The residual warmth will set it into a lacquer. Let them rest 3 minutes — this is non-negotiable.

The Finish

Jasmine rice into a wide shallow bowl. Slice the thighs against the grain into thick strips and pile them on. Drizzle media crema in a lazy spiral. Heap pickled onion on one side, scatter cilantro everywhere. Crumble queso fresco. Flaky salt. The other lime wedge on the rim. Eat it before you've finished plating the second bowl.

Pantry Notes

  • Swaps — Queso fresco running low; cotija works, just go easier on the flaky salt. No mango? A ripe peach or 2 tbsp apricot jam will pull the same trick.
  • Keeps — Glazed thighs hold 3 days, fridge. Reheat gently in a covered pan with a splash of water — high heat kills the lacquer.
  • Leftovers — Shred any cold leftover into tomorrow's tacos with the rest of the pickled onion and a fresh squeeze of lime. That's lunch.
  • This'll use most of your chipotles in adobo.

Seasonal Picks

What's peaking at the tianguis right now. Each one opens a dish.

  • Mangoes Peaking. The Ataulfo are honey-sweet — chipotle-mango glaze, mango-habanero salsa, or just sliced with tajin and lime.
  • Poblanos First of the year. Char and peel for rajas con crema, or stuff and bake.
  • Squash blossoms Starting to show. Sauté in butter with garlic, fold into a quesadilla with shredded chicken.
  • Sweet corn (elote) Early bowls coming in. Char on a dry pan, scrape off the kernels for any bowl this week.
iv.

Unlock New Dishes

Three things you don't have. Each one opens a doorway.

  • Gochujang Korean fermented chili paste. Sticky glazes, smoky-sweet braises, the kind of funk that plays beautifully with chipotle. Costco or any Asian grocer.
  • Tahini Opens the Middle Eastern shelf — drizzles, dressings, a creamy counterpoint to charred chicken. Keeps for months.
  • Salsa macha Crunchy chili-and-seed oil. Spoon it on anything — eggs, tacos, glazed thighs. A pantry multiplier.
